The knowledge base is the heart of every Canelle AI project. It's the collection of content your agent reads, understands, and draws on when answering your users' questions — so the quality and relevance of what you add directly determines how accurate and useful your agent becomes. Think of it as your agent's long-term memory: the richer and more targeted it is, the better your agent performs.

## How Indexing Works

When you add a source, Canelle AI processes it through a three-step pipeline:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Chunking">
    Canelle AI splits your content into small, overlapping segments called chunks. Each chunk represents a coherent piece of information — a paragraph, a section, or a logical unit of text.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Embedding">
    Each chunk is converted into a vector embedding — a numerical representation that captures the meaning of the text. This is what enables semantic search: your agent understands intent, not just keywords.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Storage & Retrieval">
    The embeddings are stored in a vector database. When a user asks a question, Canelle AI retrieves the most semantically relevant chunks and passes them to the AI model to generate a grounded, accurate answer.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Supported Source Types

Canelle AI supports a range of content sources to fit how your team already works. Each source type is processed slightly differently, and indexing times vary based on content length and complexity.

| Source Type | Best For | Processing Time |
|---|---|---|
| PDF & DOCX | Documentation, reports, guides | 10–30 seconds |
| URLs | Web pages, articles, online docs | 15–45 seconds |
| Notion pages | Team wikis, internal docs | 10–30 seconds |
| Confluence pages | Technical documentation | 10–30 seconds |
| Plain text | Notes, FAQs, custom content | 5–10 seconds |

## Tips for a High-Quality Knowledge Base

<Tip>
  Getting the most out of your knowledge base comes down to what you put in it:

  - **Add content your users actually ask about.** Start with your most common questions and work outward — don't just bulk-upload everything.
  - **Use descriptive filenames for PDFs.** A file named `return-policy-2024.pdf` is easier to manage and debug than `document_final_v3.pdf`.
  - **Avoid duplicate content.** If the same information appears in multiple sources, it dilutes answer quality and may cause your agent to repeat itself or give inconsistent responses. Keep one authoritative source per topic.
</Tip>
