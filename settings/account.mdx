Account settings control your personal profile, workspace members, and API access credentials. Everything you need to manage who has access to your workspace — and how external applications connect to it — lives here.

## Profile Settings

Navigate to **Settings → Profile** to update your personal information and notification preferences.

<Accordion title="Name and Email Address">
  Update your display name and the email address associated with your Canelle AI account. Changes to your email address take effect after you verify the new address via a confirmation link.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Password">
  Click **Change password**, enter your current password, then enter and confirm your new password. Your new password must be at least 8 characters long.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Notification Preferences">
  Toggle email notifications on or off for each of the following events:

  - **Low credit warnings** — receive an alert when your AI credit balance drops below a set threshold
  - **New conversations (daily digest)** — a daily summary email listing new conversations from the previous day
  - **Weekly analytics summary** — a weekly email with top-line metrics for all your projects
</Accordion>

## Team Management

Navigate to **Settings → Team** to invite collaborators and manage their access. Team management is available on **Professional** and **Ultimate** plans.

### Inviting Team Members

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Team settings page">
    Go to **Settings → Team** and click **Invite member**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter their email address">
    Type the email address of the person you want to invite, then select a role from the dropdown.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send the invitation">
    Click **Send invite**. The invitee receives an email with a link to accept and join your workspace.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Roles and Permissions

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Admin" icon="shield">
    Full access to all projects, agent settings, knowledge bases, and billing. Admins can also invite and remove other members.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Editor" icon="pen-to-square">
    Can create and edit knowledge bases and configure agent settings across all projects. Editors cannot view or change billing information.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Viewer" icon="eye">
    Read-only access to conversation history and analytics. Viewers cannot make any changes to projects or settings.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Removing a Member

To remove a team member, click the **trash icon** next to their name in the member list. Their access is revoked immediately.

## API Keys

Navigate to **Settings → API Keys** to generate credentials for connecting external applications to your Canelle AI projects.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Generate a new key">
    Click **Generate new API key**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name the key">
    Give the key a descriptive name that reflects where it will be used — for example, `Production app` or `Testing`. This label helps you identify the key later.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Copy the key immediately">
    Your full API key is displayed **once only**. Copy it now and store it securely (for example, in a secrets manager or a `.env` file). You cannot retrieve the full key again after closing this dialog.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use the key in your application">
    Pass the key in the `Authorization` header of every API request:

    ```http
    Authorization: Bearer YOUR_API_KEY
    ```
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Revoking a Key

To revoke an API key, click **Revoke** next to its name in the API Keys list. Revocation is immediate — any application using that key loses access instantly.

<Warning>
  API keys grant full access to all your projects. Store them securely and never commit them to version control. If you suspect a key has been compromised, revoke it immediately and generate a replacement.
</Warning>

<Note>
  If you lose an API key, revoke it and generate a new one. There is no way to recover the value of a key after it has been generated and the dialog closed.
</Note>
