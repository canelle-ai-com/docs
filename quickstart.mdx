This guide walks you through everything you need to go from a blank slate to a live, deployed AI agent. You'll create a project, feed it some content, and get a working chat interface — all in a single sitting.

## Build and Deploy Your First Agent

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create an Account">
    Go to [app.canelleai.com](https://app.canelleai.com) and sign up with your email address or Google account. No credit card is required to start your free trial — your account is ready the moment you confirm your email.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a Project">
    From your dashboard, click **New Project**. Give your project a clear name that reflects its purpose (for example, *Customer Support* or *Product Docs*) and add an optional description. Click **Create Project** to continue.

    Each project has its own isolated knowledge base and agent, so you can run separate assistants for different use cases without them interfering with each other.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add Knowledge">
    Open the **Knowledge Base** tab inside your project. You have several ways to add content — the three fastest are:

    <Tabs>
      <Tab title="Upload a PDF">
        Click **Upload File** and select one or more PDF documents from your computer. Canelle AI will parse, chunk, and index the content automatically. Most documents are ready to query within a minute.

        ```
        Supported: PDF, DOCX, TXT, MD
        Max file size: 50 MB per file
        ```
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="Paste a URL">
        Click **Add URL** and enter the full address of any publicly accessible webpage. Canelle AI crawls the page, extracts the readable content, and indexes it. You can add multiple URLs one at a time or in bulk.

        ```
        Example: https://docs.yourcompany.com/getting-started
        ```
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="Type or Paste Text">
        Click **Add Text** to open a plain-text editor. Type directly or paste content — meeting notes, FAQs, internal policies, anything. Give it a title so you can identify it later, then click **Save**.
      </Tab>
    </Tabs>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure Your Agent">
    Navigate to the **Agent** tab and customize how your assistant presents itself:

    - **Agent Name** — The display name users see in the chat interface (e.g., *Aria* or *Support Bot*).
    - **Welcome Message** — The opening message your agent sends when a conversation starts. Use it to set expectations: *"Hi! Ask me anything about our product docs."*
    - **Response Language** — Choose the language your agent responds in, regardless of the language used in your source documents.

    Click **Save Settings** when you're done.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Deploy Your Agent">
    Open the **Deploy** tab to get your agent in front of users.

    <Tabs>
      <Tab title="Website Widget">
        Copy the embed script snippet and paste it into the `<head>` or just before the closing `</body>` tag of your website's HTML. The chat bubble appears automatically on every page that includes the script.

        ```html
        <!-- Canelle AI Widget -->
        <script
          src="https://cdn.canelleai.com/widget.js"
          data-project-id="YOUR_PROJECT_ID"
          async
        ></script>
        ```
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="Shareable Link">
        Toggle **Enable Public Link** to on. Canelle AI generates a unique URL you can share directly with anyone — no embed required. Copy the link and send it via email, Slack, or any other channel.

        ```
        Example: https://app.canelleai.com/chat/your-project-slug
        ```
      </Tab>
    </Tabs>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Test Your Agent">
    Click **Open Preview** in the top-right corner of your project to launch a live chat sandbox. Ask a question that you know is covered by the content you indexed. You should see a response with a reference to the source it drew from.

    If the answer looks off, check that the relevant content was indexed successfully under **Knowledge Base > Sources**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Every new project starts with **30 free AI credits** so you can test your agent without committing to a paid plan. Each message your agent processes consumes one credit.
</Note>

<Tip>
  PDFs and URLs are just the beginning. Canelle AI also supports Notion, Confluence, sitemaps, and more. See the full list of supported source types in [Adding Sources](/knowledge-base/adding-sources).
</Tip>
